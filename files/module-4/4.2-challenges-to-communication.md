# Chapter 4.2 — Challenges to Communication

**Module 4: Communication for Security Professionals**

---

## Section 1: Common Communication Barriers

You may encounter barriers to effective communication, including:
- Differences in **language**
- **Cultural practices**
- **Physical or cognitive (mental) disability**

> Do not assume challenges are the result of deliberate behaviour — the individual may want to cooperate. Give them the benefit of the doubt.

---

## Section 2: Your Own Communication Challenges

If English is not your first language or you learned it outside North America, consider:

- **Speaking slowly** — accents can make quick speech difficult to understand
- **Pronouncing words carefully** and deliberately
- **Avoiding non-Canadian slang** (e.g., "flat" instead of "house" or "apartment")
- **Checking that the listener understood**, especially after giving instructions

---

## Section 3: Avoiding Bias, Discrimination, and Harassment

The first rule in overcoming barriers is avoiding communication that is biased, discriminating, or harassing. Your attitudes and beliefs have **no place in professional communication**.

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Bias** | A preference for or against some characteristic (gender, ethnicity, religion, sexual orientation) |
| **Discrimination** | Treating someone differently because of their group membership, or showing favouritism for the same reasons |
| **Harassment** | Treating someone negatively due to unrelated circumstances — also termed "bullying" |

---

## Section 4: Overcoming Communication Barriers

When barriers prevent verbal communication, **body language becomes critical**.

### Strategies

- **Maintain good posture** — shows respect for yourself and the other person
- **Be attentive** — ensure your facial gestures show you are paying attention
- **Refrain from showing frustration**, impatience, or anger
- **Use plain language** — avoid complicated words or slang
- **Stay focused** on the conversation
- **Ask one question at a time** — allow the person to answer before moving on
- **Use alternative means of communication:**
  - Written notes (e.g., for hearing-impaired individuals)
  - Gestures (e.g., holding palm outward to signal "stop," pointing at your ID card)
- **Check for understanding** — respectfully ask "Do you understand?"
- **Remain patient** — try to empathize with the other person who may be even more frustrated than you
