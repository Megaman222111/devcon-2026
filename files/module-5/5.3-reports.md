# Chapter 5.3 — Reports

**Module 5: Documentation and Evidence**

---

## Section 1: Types of Reports

As a security professional, you are required to **observe and report**. Types of reports include:

- Sign-in/sign-out sheets
- List of deliveries
- Maintenance requests
- Shift reports
- Incident reports
- Traffic collision reports

---

## Section 2: Report Writing Guidelines

Reports should be:
- **Accurate** reflections of what took place
- **Free from personal opinion** and irrelevant detail
- **Clearly written** — another person should be able to read and understand what transpired
- Written using **correct grammar** and legible handwriting

### Additional Pointers

- **Use past tense** — "called, ran, shouted, looked"
- **Think about your senses** — include what you saw, heard, smelled if relevant
- **Use short sentences** and uncomplicated language
- **Organize chronologically** — report events in the order they occurred
- **Refer to your notebook** to ensure all relevant details are captured
- **Include a diagram** if helpful
- **Have a colleague review** — ask them to check for clarity and completeness

---

## Section 3: Report Logistics

When starting at an organization, clarify:
- **Which reports** are required, and their **frequency**
- **Format** — handwritten vs. computer-generated
- **Number of copies** required
- **Submission method** — mailbox, fax, email
- **Who receives** the reports — direct supervisor, client designate
- **Retain a copy** for your own records

---

## Section 4: Traffic Collision Reports

Traffic collisions should generally be investigated by police. However, if you are present at the scene:

### Information to Collect

- Name, address, telephone numbers for each driver
- Driver licence number and insurance information
- Time, date, and exact location
- Licence plate numbers, make, model, year, and colour of vehicles
- Description of damage to vehicles
- Description of injuries to persons
- Name, address, and telephone numbers for witnesses

### Additional Documentation

- Create a **simple diagram** showing vehicle directions relative to scene features
- Note **weather conditions** at the time
- Record anything unusual (e.g., open liquor container in vehicle)
- Provide your name and contact number to drivers and any police/peace officers present

## Section 5: Mandatory Reports to the Registrar

The Security Services and Investigators Regulation requires mandatory reports:

### 24-Hour Reporting Window

| Event | Deadline |
|-------|----------|
| Charged with offence under Criminal Code or Controlled Drugs and Substances Act | Within 24 hours |
| Loss of licence | Within 24 hours |

Reports submitted on **Incident Report Form (PS3750)**.

### 30-Day Reporting Window

Changes to information provided at time of application:
- Change of name
- Change of contact information
- Related training completed
- Changes to citizenship, immigration, or work permit documents

Changes reported using **Update of Individual License Form (PS3746)**.
