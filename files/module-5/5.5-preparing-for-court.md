# Chapter 5.5 — Preparing for Court

**Module 5: Documentation and Evidence**

---

## Section 1: Being Called to Court

You will be formally notified if required to attend court via a **subpoena** — a document summoning witnesses to testify about a particular matter.

Key points:
- The subpoena specifies the **date and time** you must attend
- You can be called even if **no longer employed** with the organization from the time of the incident
- Attendance is **mandatory** — failure or refusal to attend may result in **contempt of court** charges
- Make **prior arrangements** with your supervisor if court time falls during a regular shift

---

## Section 2: Preparation

If you have kept an accurate, complete notebook and submitted professional reports, preparing for court should be manageable.

### Before Court

- **Review your notes and reports** related to the incident
- Refresh your memory on the **sequence of events**
- Ensure your **notebooks are available** to bring to court if needed
- Dress **professionally** — your appearance reflects on your credibility

---

## Section 3: Testifying

When giving testimony:

- **Tell the truth** — your credibility depends on honest, accurate testimony
- **Refer to your notebook** if permitted by the court — your notes were made at or near the time of the incident
- **Answer only what is asked** — do not volunteer extra information
- **Speak clearly** and at a pace that allows the court to follow
- If you do not understand a question, **ask for clarification**
- If you do not know the answer, **say so** — do not guess or speculate
