# Chapter 3.9 — Working with a Partner

**Module 3: Basic Security Procedures**

---

## Section 1: When You Work with a Partner

Partner assignments are more likely at:
- **Large sites or venues**
- Locations with **higher incidence of criminal activity**

### Advantages
- Better control of **large crowds** — a partner can "watch your back"
- Additional **safety** when surprising someone committing a crime
- Combined **skill sets** (first aid experience, language abilities)

---

## Section 2: Communication Guidelines

Effective, ongoing communication is key to a successful partnership.

### Best Practices

- **Always advise your partner** when leaving your post (washroom, coffee break); let them know when you return
- Ensure a **means of communication** when apart (radio or cell phone); pre-determine check-in frequency
- Establish a **protocol for danger** — identify a particular phrase for radio/phone communications that signals a problem without tipping off an intruder
- **Develop a plan** when entering a building where you suspect criminal activity:
  - Who goes through the door first?
  - Who calls for help? (Prevent both calling — or neither calling)
- **Assess each other's skills** — one may have first aid experience or speak another language

> Your partner is your back-up when things don't go as expected. Take the time to communicate so you are prepared when you need each other most.
