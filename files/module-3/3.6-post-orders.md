# Chapter 3.6 — Post Orders

**Module 3: Basic Security Procedures**

---

## Section 1: What Are Post Orders?

A post order is a document outlining your **duties and responsibilities** during your shift. Your employer provides this at the outset of your shift.

- **Read them each time** — there may be changes or updated information
- Note any questions or areas where information may be missing/outdated
- Contact your supervisor to clarify issues at the **start of your shift**

Post orders serve as a **policy and procedure guide**. You are expected to be in compliance and ensure others (public, guests) adhere to the rules.

---

## Section 2: Typical Contents of Post Orders

- Contact names and numbers for **emergency services and personnel**
- List of other **security personnel** on site and their locations/duties
- Description of where the **post is located**
- Hours and **duration of the shift**
- List of individuals/personnel **permitted access**
- How to handle tasks such as **receiving deliveries** or admitting maintenance personnel
- Procedures for **patrol** (where to patrol, frequency)
- Instructions for **locking/unlocking doors** (e.g., after certain hours)
- Instructions for **building emergencies** (power outages, equipment failure)
- Instructions for **hazards or threats** (fire, flood, accident, bomb threat)
- **Reporting requirements** for incidents, emergencies, and shift end
