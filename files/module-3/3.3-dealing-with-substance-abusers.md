# Chapter 3.3 — Dealing with Substance Abusers

**Module 3: Basic Security Procedures**

---

## Section 1: Understanding Substance Abuse

Substance abuse occurs in all communities. An individual is considered addicted to a substance when they:

- Give up activities, hobbies, friends, and relationships to spend time using/abusing a substance
- Continue to use even if doing so has negative effects on well-being (health, financial, relationships)
- Experience **physical symptoms when they stop** (withdrawal)

---

## Section 2: Common Situations

As a security professional, you will encounter substance abusers in situations such as:

- **Loitering** — hanging around with fellow users or waiting to buy more drugs
- **Shoplifting** — stealing items to sell for drugs, or stealing necessities they can no longer afford
- **Creating a disturbance** — being drunk or high and not in control
- **Trespassing** — entering premises looking for drugs, alcohol, or money
- **Break and enter** — with intent to steal money or items to obtain more substances

---

## Section 3: Signs and Behaviours

These indicators may suggest substance abuse — or may be symptoms of another problem. It is **not your responsibility** to diagnose or treat addiction; however, recognizing common signs helps your interactions.

| Physical | Behavioural | Psychological |
|----------|------------|---------------|
| Bloodshot eyes | Poor attendance at work/school | Sudden personality/attitude change |
| Pupils larger or smaller than usual | Unexplained need for money; may borrow or steal | Mood swings, irritability, angry outbursts |
| Changes in appetite or sleep patterns; sudden weight change | Secretive or suspicious behaviour | Hyperactivity, agitation, giddiness |
| Deteriorated appearance, poor grooming | Sudden change in friends, hangouts, hobbies | Lack of motivation |
| Unusual smells on breath, body, or clothing | Getting into fights, accidents, or illegal activities | Lethargic, "spaced out" |
| Tremors, slurred speech, poor coordination | | Fearful, anxious, paranoid for no reason |

---

## Section 4: Responding to Substance Abusers

Dealing with someone who is drunk or high may not be easy:
- It may not be possible to **reason with them**
- They may be **incapable of understanding** or following instructions

### Steps
1. Ask the person to **stop the behaviour** or **leave the premises**
2. If they refuse, **call the police** to intervene
3. If someone appears to be having a **health emergency** from drug or alcohol use, **call EMS immediately**

---

## Section 5: Identifying Drug Paraphernalia

Drug paraphernalia refers to items used to package, make, use, or conceal illegal drugs.

### Packaging
- Small plastic bags, glass vials, small flaps of shiny paper, tiny plastic sacks ("8-balls")
- Prescription drugs may be in original packaging (tablets, capsules, liquid)
- "Club drugs" are sold as tablets made to look like candy

### Items for Use
- Metal foil, "roach" clips, glass or metal smoking pipes
- Broken light bulbs, bottle caps, pop cans
- Syringes, spoons (for heating), elastics/rubber ties/surgical tubing (for injection drug users)
- Razor blades, small mirrors, tiny "coke" spoons, thin straws (for cocaine users)
