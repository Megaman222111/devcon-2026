# Chapter 5.4 — Evidence

**Module 5: Documentation and Evidence**

---

## Section 1: What Is Evidence?

Evidence is the information used by investigators and court personnel to **confirm or eliminate** an individual's participation in a criminal act. Evidence may be:

- **Verbal** (testimony)
- **Physical** (objects found at the scene)
- **Documentary** (emails, letters, photographs)

Evidence must be **collected and protected** so there is no opportunity for tampering.

---

## Section 2: First Responder Duties

As a security professional, you may be the first person at a crime scene. Your duties:

1. Ensure there are **no further hazards** at the scene (broken glass, chemicals)
2. Check for the **safety of bystanders**
3. Call **emergency services and/or police** if not already done
4. **Observe the scene** using all your senses — make mental notes and record in your notebook
5. **Remove extra persons** (curious onlookers) from the scene
6. Ask **witnesses** to step aside and await further instructions
7. **Protect the scene** — prevent people from walking through or cleaning up

---

## Section 3: When Police Arrive

- Note the **time of their arrival** (include in notebook and reports)
- **Introduce yourself** to the officer taking charge
- Answer any questions the officer may have
- Let the officer know if there are **witnesses**
- Get the officer's **name and contact information** for your report
- Document the **transition of authority** — this may need to be demonstrated in court

---

## Section 4: Types of Evidence

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Direct** | First-hand testimony by a witness | A witness testifying about what they saw |
| **Circumstantial** | Suggests someone may have been present or responsible | An ID card found on the floor of a broken-into office |
| **Hearsay** | Based on something said by a third party | Overhearing a threat and testifying to it |
| **Documentary** | Photographs, security footage, documents | CCTV video of an incident |
| **Physical** | Objects found at the scene | A crowbar found at a break-in location |
| **Trace** | Fingerprints, footprints, DNA samples | Usually invisible to the naked eye; collected by trained experts |
