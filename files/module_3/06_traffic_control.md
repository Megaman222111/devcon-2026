# Traffic Control

Security professionals may be required to direct traffic on private property as directed by their employer and the client; you should not be directing traffic on public roads, unless directed to do so by a police officer. You should advise your supervisor if you are asked to leave your post in order to assist a police officer with traffic control; someone will need to assume your duties.

Manual traffic control is generally required at large venues, such as sporting arenas or shopping malls, or on busy construction sites. Traffic can refer to vehicles (including bicycles), pedestrians, or both. When directing traffic, keep in mind

• Your own personal safety
• The safety of motorists and pedestrians
• Professional deportment

• Providing clear direction to both motorist and pedestrian traffic
