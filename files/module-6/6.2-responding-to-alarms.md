# Chapter 6.2 — Responding to Alarms

**Module 6: Emergency Response Procedures for Security Professionals**

---

## Section 1: Know Your Alarms

If alarms are in place at your work site, find out:

- Which alarms you are **responsible for**
- The **required response** for each
- The **location** of alarms and control panels
- What each alarm **monitors**
- **Acceptable thresholds** (for building systems) — record in your notebook

> Each site will likely use **different alarm response protocols**. Do not assume protocols will be the same even among locations operated by the same organization.

---

## Section 2: General Alarm Response Protocol

When an alarm is triggered:

1. **Respond to ALL alarms** — always start with the assumption the alarm is genuine, even if false alarms are common
2. **Contact your supervisor** — advise you are leaving your post and report the alarm
3. **Determine the source** and appropriate response:
   - **False alarm** — follow post order protocol; report back to supervisor
   - **Emergency** — follow emergency post order protocols
4. **Manage the scene** appropriately:
   - Fires and other emergencies — facilitate **orderly evacuation**
   - Robbery or child abduction — **seal off exit doors**
   - Building systems failure — remain calm, advise persons to be patient

---

## Section 3: Alarm System Shutdown

You may be requested to shut down alarm sections for maintenance or construction work. Important rules:

- **Do not shut down any alarm** without clear direction (post orders, supervisor instruction, or emergency services direction)
- In non-emergency settings, **clarify requests** with your supervisor before taking action
- **Document** any shutdown or alarm testing in your notebook, including the name and contact information of the person giving instructions

---

## Section 4: Off-Site Alarm Response

When dispatched to an off-site alarm:

1. **Obey all traffic laws** — you may not speed or ignore traffic signals
2. Upon arrival, check if **doors and windows are secure**
3. If you see evidence of criminal activity, **notify police immediately** — document findings, do not displace or destroy evidence
4. Be **continually observant**
5. If premises are unsecured, **notify police** for their advice
6. Follow protocol for advising the **alarm key-holder** to reset the system
7. **Document events** in your notebook and prepare reports
