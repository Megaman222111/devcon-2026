# Chapter 5.2 — Statements

**Module 5: Documentation and Evidence**

---

## Section 1: What Is a Statement?

A statement is an explanation of what an individual observed or recalls about a specific event, told through either a formal or informal interview.

Statements can be:
- **Simple:** "I saw a person kicking in the back door of the building as I was driving by."
- **Elaborate:** A detailed explanation of events surrounding an emergency, such as a gas line explosion

---

## Section 2: Who Provides Statements?

Statements may be given by anyone with information about an event:
- Witnesses
- Employees
- Persons suspected of engaging in criminal activity

---

## Section 3: Your Role in Gathering Statements

- Your role is to **observe and report** — you are often the first person on scene
- It is appropriate to **make notes** about what happened
- **Do not** suggest or imply you are a police officer
- Be clear about your role and your reasons for collecting information
- Avoid using the word "statement" — instead say: *"I will need to prepare a report about this incident; will you please tell me what happened?"*

---

## Section 4: What to Ask

Use the same **who, what, where, when, why, and how** framework from your notebook training. As always:

- Write down **facts**, not opinions or unproven conclusions
- Share information with police for their investigation
