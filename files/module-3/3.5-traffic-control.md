# Chapter 3.5 — Traffic Control

**Module 3: Basic Security Procedures**

---

## Section 1: Overview

Security professionals may be required to direct traffic on **private property** as directed by their employer and client. You should **not** direct traffic on public roads unless directed to do so by a police officer.

> If asked to leave your post to assist a police officer with traffic control, advise your supervisor — someone will need to assume your duties.

Traffic control is generally required at:
- Large venues (sporting arenas, shopping malls)
- Busy construction sites

When directing traffic, keep in mind:
- Your own **personal safety**
- The safety of **motorists and pedestrians**
- Professional **deportment**
- Providing **clear direction**

---

## Section 2: Staying Safe

### Visibility and Preparedness
- Wear a **reflective vest**
- Carry a **lighted baton or flashlight**
- Use a **whistle**
- Dress appropriately for weather conditions (warm clothing in winter, hat and water in summer)

### Positioning
- Be visible to traffic from **all directions**
- In an intersection, the **middle** may be safest
- In parking lots, stand near the **middle of the roadway** where inbound and outbound traffic meet
- Use **traffic cones or barricades** to help funnel traffic
- **Establish eye contact** with drivers before signaling — do not assume you have been seen

---

## Section 3: Traffic Direction Signals

### STOP
1. Identify the vehicle you need to stop
2. Make eye contact with the driver
3. Extend your arm, point to the driver, then lift your palm facing toward the driver
4. Hold your palm toward the driver as long as you wish them to remain stopped

### GO
1. Identify the vehicle you want to proceed
2. Make eye contact with the driver
3. Extend your arm toward the driver, bend at the elbow, bringing your palm toward you (come forward signal)
4. Maintain palm extended to drivers from other directions (indicating stop)

### TURN RIGHT OR LEFT
1. Identify the vehicle you want to turn
2. Make eye contact with the driver
3. Point arm in the direction you want the driver to turn, bending slightly at the elbow
4. Maintain palm up and outward to traffic from other directions

### PEDESTRIAN TRAFFIC
1. Make eye contact with the pedestrian
2. Keep pedestrians off the road until traffic has moved or is stopped
3. Use the same hand signals as for vehicles

---

## Section 4: Whistle Signals

| Signal | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| One long blast | Stop |
| Two short blasts | Proceed |
| Short, rapid blasts | Warning |

> **Emergency vehicles** have right of way over all other traffic. Assist their arrival by bringing all traffic to a stop until all emergency vehicles have passed.
