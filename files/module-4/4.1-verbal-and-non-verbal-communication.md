# Chapter 4.1 — Verbal and Non-Verbal Communication

**Module 4: Communication for Security Professionals**

---

## Section 1: Verbal Communication

As a security professional, you will engage in conversations:
- In person
- By telephone
- Over the radio

Your behaviour — including your verbal communication style — should be a **positive reflection** on the company and industry you represent.

### Professional Verbal Communication Is:

- **Clearly understood** — speak at a rate that can be understood; enunciate your words
- **Courteous and respectful** — say please and thank you when appropriate
- **Free from slang, racial slurs, and profanity**
- **Direct and to the point**
- **In plain language** — free from complicated words or unique acronyms

---

## Section 2: Tone and Volume

Your tone and volume send a message to the person(s) you are speaking to.

| Use This | Not This |
|----------|----------|
| **Confident** — calm and collected; assures the listener you know what you are talking about | **Arrogant** — trying to show power over the individual; causes resentment |
| **Friendly** — approachable; people are more likely to bring concerns to your attention | **Dismissive** — showing disinterest; "brushing off" the individual |
| **Assertive** — making requests in a confident, straightforward manner | **Aggressive** — forcing your request or opinion; viewed as forceful behaviour |

### Volume Guidelines

- Use a **normal, conversational voice** in most interactions
- Speaking **too quietly** suggests timidity — trespassers may not take you seriously
- **Shouting** is perceived as threatening or aggressive
- Only raise your voice when speaking from a distance or competing with environmental noise

## Section 3: Non-Verbal Communication

Studies suggest that as much as **90% of meaning** comes through non-verbal means. Non-verbal communication has four components:

### Visual (Body Language)
- Facial expressions
- Posture
- Eye contact/movement
- Gestures

### Tactile (Touch)
- Handshakes
- Pat on the back
- Hugs

### Vocal (Tone of Voice)
Can suggest: a question, disbelief, seriousness, excitement, or anger

### Use of Time, Space, and Image
- Being on time vs. being late
- Standing in another person's "bubble"
- The way we dress

> You will most likely engage in non-verbal communication with other individuals **before you ever say a word.**

### Your Appearance Communicates

A clean, neatly pressed uniform, good posture, and an engaged facial expression send a message that you are actively on duty. People will form opinions about you and security professionals in general based on your non-verbal behaviour.

> When approached for help — even beyond the scope of your duties — provide a polite response. Do not roll your eyes, sigh, allow your shoulders to droop, or use an annoyed tone.

## Section 4: The LEAPS Method

A communication style known as **Verbal Judo** uses the LEAPS method for gaining cooperation and compliance.

### L — Listen
Be an **active listener**:
- Make eye contact with the speaker
- Nod or acknowledge points to show you're paying attention
- Do not "tune out" by looking around or checking your phone
- Do not interrupt — wait for a pause to ask for clarification

### E — Empathize
Try to understand the situation **from their perspective** (this is not the same as agreeing with them).

> **Example:** Someone tries to cross a fire perimeter because they believe a friend is inside. You would say: "I understand you are probably very worried about your friend. If I let you go inside, I could be putting you in danger. The best way you can help is to wait out here, where it is safe."

### A — Ask
Make sure you understand what the other person is saying:
- Do not make the person feel stupid — try: "Will you please say that again? I was unable to understand you clearly."
- Be specific about what information you need clarified

### P — Paraphrase
Take what the speaker said and **repeat it back using your own words**. This shows you were listening and allows you to double-check understanding.

### S — Summarize
At the end of the conversation, summarize what was discussed, including any action steps. This makes expectations clear and signals that the matter is resolved.

> **Example:** "Just so we are both clear, I am not able to allow you entrance because you do not have the proper ticket. You are going to call your girlfriend to bring your ticket. Once I confirm you have the right ticket, I will admit you."
