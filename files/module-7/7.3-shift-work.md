# Chapter 7.3 — Shift Work

**Module 7: Health & Safety for Security Professionals**

---

## Section 1: The Impact of Shift Work

Security patrols are often required during evening and night hours. Common negative effects of shift work include:

- Disruption of the **circadian (sleep) rhythm**
- **Sleep deprivation**
- Disorders of the **gastrointestinal and cardiovascular** systems
- Worsening of **existing disorders**
- Disruption of **family and social life**

---

## Section 2: Health and Nutrition

Shift work disrupts normal routines, including meal times. Recommended strategies:

- **Afternoon workers** — have your main meal in the middle of the day, not the middle of your shift
- **Night workers** — eat lightly throughout the shift and have a moderate breakfast
- **Relax during meals** and allow time for digestion
- Drink **lots of water**
- Cut back on **highly salted foods**
- Reduce foods **high in fat**
- Maintain **regular eating patterns** with well-balanced meals
- Eat the usual balance of **vegetables, fruit, lean meat, poultry, fish, dairy, grains, and bread**
- Avoid excessive use of **antacids, tranquilizers, and sleeping pills**
- Minimize **caffeine and alcohol**
- Avoid **fast food and vending machines**

---

## Section 3: Physical Fitness

Fitness contributes to your ability to remain **focused and alert** on the job and sustains you through long shifts.

### Strategies for Staying Alert During Late Shifts

- Try to **exercise during breaks**
- **Talk with co-workers** while you work
- Try to work with a **"buddy"**
- Take short breaks to **walk, climb stairs**, or use the lounge
- Don't leave the most **tedious tasks** to the end of your shift
- Exchange ideas with colleagues on **coping with shift work**

> **Important:** Avoid exercising **before going to bed** — exercise raises energy and body temperature. Allow at least **three hours** between exercising and sleeping.

---

## Section 4: Sleep

Shift work's most obvious impact is disruption to **regular sleep patterns**. Suggestions for better rest:

- Make sure family and friends are **aware of and respectful** of your sleep hours
- Ensure a **comfortable, quiet place** to sleep during the day
- Use **air conditioning, answering machines, ear plugs, and good window coverings**
- Make time for **quiet relaxation before bed** (reading, breathing exercises, muscle relaxation)
- Sleep on a **set schedule** to establish a routine
- Avoid **strenuous exercise before sleeping** — your metabolism stays elevated for hours
- If you don't fall asleep after one hour, **read a book** or listen to quiet music
- If sleep still doesn't come, **reschedule your sleeping hours** for later in the day

---

## Section 5: Social Life

Working shifts can cause you to lose contact with family and friends, leading to feelings of isolation.

### Strategies for Maintaining Social Life

- Schedule at least **one daily meal with your family**
- **Socialize with other shift workers** and their families
- Keep in touch with your **spouse/partner and children daily**
- Set time aside for **just you and your spouse/partner**
- **Plan family activities carefully** — plan days off in advance if possible
- Practice **stress reduction**
- Use a **calendar** to schedule events
- Try to **prioritize tasks** and tackle one at a time

> Working shift work will cause challenges, but implementing these strategies should help ease concerns and protect your health and well-being on and off the job.
