# Chapter 2.3 — Additional Legislation

**Module 2: The Canadian Legal System and Security Professionals**

---

## Section 1: Overview

The job description for a security professional can be quite varied. Some duties are rooted in other pieces of legislation beyond the Criminal Code. Throughout your career, you will commonly deal with issues arising from:

- Gaming and Liquor Act and Regulation
- Trespass to Premises Act
- Petty Trespass Act
- Employment Standards Code and Regulation

---

## Section 2: Gaming and Liquor Act

A security professional **does not have authority** under the Gaming and Liquor Act; however, you may be dispatched to a post where gaming or liquor is present, such as:
- Sports stadiums
- Private functions

### ProServe and ProTect Training

> **ProServe:** As of January 1, 2010, if your job involves liquor sales, service, or security in Alberta, you must have ProServe training.

> **ProTect:** Training to improve observation and communication skills, helping security staff prevent, defuse, and manage customer disturbances before violence escalates.

These are requirements of the **Alberta Gaming and Liquor Commission (AGLC)**. Bouncers are not required to be licensed under SSIA; however, overlap may occur at mixed-use facilities (convention centres, hotels, etc.).

### Key Point on Liquor Seizure

While guarding access to a venue, you may deny entrance to anyone possessing alcohol, but you are **not authorized to seize the liquor**. Communicate clearly that the individual has a choice: dispose of the liquor, or choose not to enter.

## Section 3: Trespass to Premises Act

This Act provides authorization for you — as a representative of the owner — to enforce trespass provisions.

### Section 2 — Trespass

No person shall trespass on premises where they have had **notice not to trespass**. Notice may be given:

- **(a)** Orally or in writing by the owner or authorized representative
- **(b)** By signs visibly displayed at each entrance normally used and at all fence corners

A person is **deemed to have had notice** when signs are properly displayed.

### Section 4 — Liability of Driver

When a trespass is committed by motor vehicle, the **driver** is guilty and liable to the fine.

### Section 5 — Arrest Without Warrant

A trespasser may be apprehended without warrant by:
- **(a)** Any peace officer, **or**
- **(b)** The owner or authorized representative of the owner

> When a non-peace-officer apprehends a trespasser, they must deliver that person to a peace officer **as soon as practicable**.

## Section 4: Petty Trespass Act

### Section 2(1) — Prohibition

Every person who:
- **(a)** enters land without permission when entry is prohibited, **or**
- **(b)** does not leave land immediately after being directed to do so by the owner, occupier, or authorized person

is **guilty of an offence**.

### Section 2.1 — Notice

Entry on land may be prohibited by notice. Entry is automatically prohibited (without posted notice) on land that is:
- A lawn, garden, or under cultivation
- Surrounded by a fence, natural boundary, or combination
- Enclosed in a manner indicating the owner's intention to keep persons off

Notice may be given:
- Orally
- In writing
- By posters or signboards at all access points and corners

> As a security professional, you may **verbally tell persons** they are not permitted to enter an area. If they ignore this notice and enter, they are guilty of an offence under the Act.

> Removing, damaging, or defacing trespass signage is an offence punishable by a fine up to **$2,000**.

## Section 5: Employment Standards Code and Regulation

The Employment Standards Code is the standard by which employers in Alberta must administer employee relationships.

This section is geared toward your **employment as a security professional** rather than legislation you enforce. As an employee, you are entitled to provisions such as:

- Being paid at least **minimum wage**
- **Compensation for overtime** hours worked

The Code establishes the **rights of workers** within Alberta. Key portions relevant to security professionals are provided in Appendix A of the participant manual.
