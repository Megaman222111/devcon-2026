# Chapter 3.10 — Use of Force Response

**Module 3: Basic Security Procedures**

---

## Section 1: Critical Thinking and Force

A key ability is applying **critical thinking** to situations as they arise. Your job is to **observe and report** — carefully consider whether calling police will suffice before stepping in on your own.

### Section 25(1), Criminal Code — Recap

As a security professional, you are addressed in:
- **s. 25(1)(a)** — as a private person
- **s. 25(1)(c)** — in aid of a peace officer

You are justified in using **as much force as is necessary** for your authorized purpose, provided you act on **reasonable grounds**.

> **80% of situations** can be addressed without using force. The majority of the time, effective verbal and non-verbal communication will bring an individual into compliance.

## Section 2: Factors in Deciding to Use Force

### How Prepared Are You?

- **Age, fitness, and strength** — how do you compare to the individual?
- **Injuries or health concerns** — how will these affect your ability? What risks do they pose?
- **Fatigue** — are you near the end of an overnight shift? Are you mentally alert?
- **Skills** — have you completed use of force training? Are you certified for equipment (baton)?
- **Confidence** — how confident are you in your ability?

> **NOTE:** If required to carry equipment such as a baton, you must take appropriate training and certification. Your employer must approve you to carry the equipment.

### What is the Situation?

- **Threats to safety** — is an individual threatening the safety of others or yourself?
- **Urgency** — you may not have time for a full assessment when there is an immediate threat
- **Environment** — consider lighting, weather, surface conditions (slippery, at height), and presence of bystanders

### What Do You Know About the Subject?

- **Weapons** — is the subject in possession of a weapon or an object that could be used as one?
- **Age, fitness, strength** — what can you observe about the individual?
- **Mental state** — does the subject appear affected by an altered mental state?
- **Under the influence** — does the subject appear impaired by drugs or alcohol?

## Section 3: The Decision Process

```
Assess the Behaviour
        |
Are verbal/non-verbal tactics resolving it?
       / \
     Yes   No
      |      |
  Continue   Is force necessary?
  without        / \
  force        No   Yes
               |      |
           Continue   Use ONLY enough
           without    force as necessary
           force           |
                      Stop using force when
                      subject is no longer
                      resisting or using force
```

Using force is a very serious matter. You will need to:
- **Justify** your actions
- **Document** the events
- **Respond** to questions and challenges that may arise

## Section 4: Excited Delirium

A rare medical condition causing individuals to behave in a very disorderly and violent manner. It is an **emergency situation** that can lead to death.

> **Call for emergency assistance immediately** if you believe an individual is in a state of excited delirium.

### Indicators
- Unusual physical strength
- Bizarre or aggressive behaviour, violence
- Overheating and sweating
- Aggression and shouting (nonsense)
- Paranoia

### Causes
- Illegal drug use (particularly cocaine)
- Heart-related disease and conditions
- Anti-psychotic medication
- Metabolic acidosis

### Restraint Safety

If you must restrain an individual:
- **Do not** position them in a way that puts pressure on their chest (interferes with breathing)
- **Do not** push their face down, blocking airways (mouth and nose)
- Have them **seated in a chair** or propped against a wall
- Remember: you are **obligated to turn them over to police** as soon as possible
