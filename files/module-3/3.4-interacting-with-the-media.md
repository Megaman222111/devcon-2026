# Chapter 3.4 — Interacting with the Media

**Module 3: Basic Security Procedures**

---

## Section 1: Your Role with the Media

Your primary duty is to safeguard persons or property as directed by your employer. On occasion, incidents that attract media attention may occur at your premises.

Unless you receive instructions stating otherwise, treat the media the **same as any other visitor**. Access may only be granted as per your employer's directive.

---

## Section 2: When Media Ask You Questions

If the media fails to gain access to the subject of their story, they may approach you with questions. It is **not your place to comment**, regardless of what you may know. You are being paid to protect the individual from intrusions such as unsolicited media attention.

---

## Section 3: Best Practices

1. **Refer the media** to a media contact, if there is one
2. **Refrain from participating** in any interviews or answering questions not related to your duties
3. **Do not be rude** — remain courteous and ask them to leave, reminding them they are on private property
4. **Do not use "no comment"** — media may take this to mean you have an opinion and possibly use it against you or the client
