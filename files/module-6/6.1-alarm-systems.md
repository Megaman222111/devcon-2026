# Chapter 6.1 — Alarm Systems

**Module 6: Emergency Response Procedures for Security Professionals**

---

## Section 1: What Is an Alarm Monitoring System?

An alarm monitoring system is a series of devices that warns occupants and other individuals that an event outside of the "norm" is taking place. Alarm systems are physically attached within buildings and can monitor for events such as fire or entry by an intruder.

### What Alarms Monitor

- Fire (smoke)
- Intruders
- Temperature
- Humidity
- Toxic substances (e.g., carbon monoxide)
- Water pressure, water leaks
- Pressure
- Equipment operation

Some alarm systems **anticipate emergencies** (intruder, fire) while others **monitor building operations** (temperature, humidity).

---

## Section 2: How Alarms Work

An alarm system has three primary components:

### 1. Sensor
"Senses" changes in the environment — movement, light, temperature, smoke, pressure

### 2. Transmitter
Sends the message from the sensor to the control panel using hard wires, telephone lines, radio signals, or wireless technology

### 3. Control Panel
Receives the information sent by the transmitter and initiates a response sequence

---

## Section 3: Types of Sensors

| Sensor Type | How It Works |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Photo sensors** | Responds to visible or invisible light. A beam across a doorway triggers a response when broken by a person entering. |
| **Ultrasonic sensors** | Works on the principle of radar — emits pulses, measures return time. If return time shortens, movement is detected. |
| **Magnetic sensors** | Magnets placed on two surfaces (e.g., window and frame) create a magnetic field. When the window opens, the field breaks, triggering an alarm. |
| **Shock sensors** | A wire around a perimeter (e.g., window) forms a complete circuit. When opened, the wire breaks, interrupting the circuit. |
| **Fire alarms** | May use photo sensors (smoke breaks light beam), temperature sensors (sudden heat increase), or ionization technology (air quality change). |
| **Gauges** | Indicators with markings showing normal vs. unsafe zones. Associated with building mechanical systems. |
| **Mechanical** | Manual devices such as pull-down fire alarm handles in schools and public buildings. |

---

## Section 4: Transmitters

An alarm transmitter relays information from the sensor to the control panel:

- **Telephone wires** — initiates a call to the monitoring company, police, or both
- **Hard-wired** — physical connection between sensor and transmitter
- **Wireless** — similar to Wi-Fi; transmitter sends information wirelessly to the control panel

---

## Section 5: Control Panels

A control panel may be:

- **Integrated** — sensor, transmitter, and control panel all in one unit (e.g., a home smoke detector that emits a loud sound)
- **Wall-mounted panel** — resembles a schematic drawing with small lights at various locations. Lights may blink, be dim, or change colour (green to red) when an alarm triggers.

Different manufacturers have different configurations of lights, drawings, indicators, and sounds.
