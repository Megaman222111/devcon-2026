# Chapter 3.7 — Starting a Shift

**Module 3: Basic Security Procedures**

---

## Section 1: Personal Preparation

Ensure you are physically and mentally prepared **before arriving** at your job site:

- **Proper rest** — get enough sleep
- **Feeling well** — going to work ill is a hazard to yourself and others
- **Clear your mind** of non-work distractions
- **Uniform** — clean, neat, and complete (don't forget name badge or licence)
- **Equipment** — notebook, pen, radio/cell phone charged and working

---

## Section 2: Shift Handover

Review the post orders with the individual you are **relieving**. Ask about:

- Any **incidents** from the previous shift
- Anything specific to watch for (e.g., inclement weather, broken window locks)
- **Personnel changes** (someone went home sick, new employee on site)

---

## Section 3: Planning Your Shift

Once expectations are clarified:

- Note the **frequency of patrols** and determine when to conduct each one
- Plan your **route** for each patrol — make a mental list of items to check
- **Change your routine** to make actions less predictable to potential intruders
- **Familiarize yourself with the site** — locate exits, escape routes, stairs
- If new to a site, **arrange a tour** before your first shift (accompany another professional on their patrol)

---

## Section 4: Site Awareness

Make yourself aware of:

- **Water supply valves** and emergency equipment (first aid kits)
- **"High risk" locations** — vaults, petty cash boxes, filing rooms, computer server rooms
- Your post orders will explain what your **priorities** should be

---

## Section 5: Check In

If required:
- Check in with **central dispatch** or your supervisor
- Advise them you are **on duty** at your post
- Provide any information they may need (e.g., outdated post orders)
- Ask questions about shift expectations
- Make entries in your **notebook** as appropriate
